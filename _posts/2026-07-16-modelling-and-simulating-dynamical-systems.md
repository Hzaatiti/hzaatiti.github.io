Welcome, and thanks for stopping by. This blog is where I plan to write about the work I care about: understanding, modelling and simulating complex dynamical systems.

Over the past decade I have moved across quite different industries, but the thread connecting them has stayed the same. At the CEA I worked on the formal verification of hybrid systems, proving safety properties of models that mix discrete logic with continuous dynamics. At IRT SystemX I helped architect autonomous shuttles and studied how to learn representations of complex systems on manifolds, together with Stanford and Inria. At Airbus Defence and Space I managed system models for large defence programs and taught model based systems engineering. Today, at NYU Abu Dhabi, I bring the same modelling mindset to neuroscience, building acquisition and analysis pipelines for magnetoencephalography and electroencephalography.

What ties this together is a simple belief: if you can write down a faithful model of a system, you can reason about it, simulate it, and trust it. Whether the system is a metro line, a drone, or the human brain, the questions are surprisingly similar. How do you capture the right level of abstraction? How do you validate the model against reality? How do you keep it maintainable as it grows?

I will use this space to share notes on those questions, on the tools I use, and on the projects I am building in the open. You can find a full account of my background on my [CV](/cv/), and my code on [GitHub](https://github.com/Hzaatiti).
